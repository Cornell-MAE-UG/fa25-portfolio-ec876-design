## Device Description

The device used in this analysis is an everyday electric pressure cooker. The device has an inner pot, a heating element, and valves. It allows for cooking of food and water at elevated pressures and temperatures.

## Operating Principle
When the pressure cooker is plugged into the wall, and turned on, eletric energy is turned into thermal energy. This energy is transferred into the food inside the pot, increasing temperature, leading to vapor formation and a corresponding rise in pressure governed by the saturation properties of water.
